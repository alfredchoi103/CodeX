# Course Framework Thread

## Main idea

The course is mainly about how to analyze public policy through `policy mix`, `tool layering`, and `effectiveness assessment`.

## Core theoretical line

- Do not analyze a single policy in isolation.
- Treat policy as a mix of instruments.
- Separate instruments into layers:
  - substantive legal-regulatory tools
  - procedural tools
  - implementation / organizational tools
  - informational / nodality tools
- Focus on the interactions among tools:
  - complementarity
  - conflict / trade-off
  - overlap / redundancy
  - gap
- Evaluate the mix with an effectiveness lens:
  - problem-solving effectiveness
  - implementation and operational effectiveness
  - policy coherence and side-effect management
  - adaptive / governance effectiveness

## What this means for the Hong Kong flooding case

- The case is not just an engineering issue.
- It is a governance and policy design issue.
- The key tension is between flood drainage logic and water-quality control logic.
- The case is suitable because it shows mixed instruments, sectoral intersections, and possible implementation gaps.

## What to keep in mind

- Complexity is normal, not exceptional.
- Policy failure may come from interaction problems rather than the absence of policy.
- Cross-department coordination matters as much as formal legal authority.
